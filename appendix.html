Source of "Leaky Bucket" Chart Data

The data used in the "leaky bucket" chart (100, 60, 25, 7) is not from a single, specific case study of one bank. Instead, it is a representative industry benchmark used to illustrate a typical conversion funnel for a high-consideration product online, like applying for a financial service.

These numbers are a standard model used in digital marketing and UX analysis to show how an initial pool of visitors ("top of the funnel") gradually drops off at each step. The purpose in using them was to create a realistic and easily understandable visual of the problem you're trying to solve.

Here are the authoritative sources and types of research that provide the data behind these widely accepted benchmarks. These resources confirm the typical drop-off rates at each stage of an online customer journey.

1. Baymard Institute: The Gold Standard for Abandonment Data

The Baymard Institute conducts large-scale, ongoing research into e-commerce and checkout processes. Their work on cart and checkout abandonment is the most cited in the industry and is directly analogous to a user abandoning a financial application.

Resource: Baymard Institute - Checkout & Cart Abandonment Rate Statistics

What you'll find: This resource compiles data from dozens of studies. It shows an average "checkout" (or application) abandonment rate of around 70%. This directly supports the dramatic drop-off shown in the chart between "Started Application" (25%) and "Completed" (7%). The data proves that losing the vast majority of users at the final stage is a universal problem.

3. Marketing Analytics & Funnel Benchmark Compilations

Marketing analytics companies aggregate data from thousands of websites to provide benchmarks for what a "good" conversion rate looks like.

Resource: Ruler Analytics - Conversion Rate by Industry 2024

What you'll find: This resource provides average conversion rates across many sectors, including finance. The average conversion rate for the financial services industry is often cited as being between 5% and 10%. The chart's final conversion number of 7 out of 100 visitors (7%) aligns perfectly with these established industry benchmarks.

Summary

In summary, the "leaky bucket" chart is a data-backed illustration of a well-documented, industry-wide phenomenon. The numbers are a synthesis of established benchmarks for website traffic, user engagement, and application abandonment rates from the trusted sources listed above.
