The Power of Peak Interest: Grabbing Attention

In a crowded digital world, attention is a currency we must earn. The key is not to interrupt, but to engage at the moment a user is most curious.

Earning, Not Demanding, Attention

As marketing principles from books like "Earn It" suggest, the most effective engagement feels helpful, not intrusive. An immediate pop-up can be annoying and counterproductive. However, a strategically timed widget that appears after a user has shown interest (e.g., after 5-7 seconds of reading) is perceived as a helpful guide.

This brief delay respects the user's initial exploration and offers value at the precise moment they are most receptive, dramatically increasing the chances of a positive interaction and conversion.

The Implementation Roadmap

Once we are alligned to move forward, I propose a strategic program to incrementally build, deploy, and automate our proactive engagement toolkit.

Foundational Promo Widget (Quick Win)

The first step is to build a robust, reusable promotional widget component. We will begin by using personalization, such as targeting new versus returning visitors, and manually configuring it to display our bank's existing, high-priority offers (e.g., a new checking account bonus) on key landing pages like the homepage. This initial step will allow us to prove the core concept and measure the immediate lift in engagement and conversions with minimal complexity.

Primary Goal: Deploy the core widget technology and establish a baseline for its ROI on a limited set of pages.

Interactive Tools Deployment

Building on the foundation, we will expand our toolkit to include interactive utility widgets. This involves developing tools like the Savings Calculator, GIC Rate Viewer, and Mortgage Calculators. We will strategically deploy these widgets on their most relevant, high-traffic pages—for example, placing the savings calculator directly on the savings account product page. This shifts our strategy from purely promotional to providing tangible value, building trust, and capturing high-intent leads.

Primary Goal: Increase user engagement and lead quality by providing valuable, context-specific interactive tools.

CMS & Component Library Integration

To ensure scalability and empower our marketing teams, we will integrate all our widgets into our Contentful CMS via our Next.js component library. This critical step transforms the widgets from developer-dependent features into reusable, configurable components. The marketing and content teams will be able to easily add, remove, and customize widgets on any page directly through the CMS, allowing for rapid campaign deployment and A/B testing without engineering overhead.

Primary Goal: Democratize widget deployment to increase marketing agility and ensure brand consistency across the site.

Automated, Smart Engine SEO Powered

Now we will introduce an intelligent automation. We will develop a smart engine that runs on the client-side. It will read the key SEO elements of any given page (such as the meta title, H1 tag, and primary keywords). It will then match this contextual data against a centralized database of our existing promotional offers and automatically display the most relevant promo in the widget. This automates personalization at scale, ensuring that a user on a business banking page sees a business credit card offer, while a user on a student page sees a student account offer.

Primary Goal: Automate the delivery of hyper-relevant offers across the entire website to maximize conversion rates.

Web Push Notification Implementation

Our end goal is to build a channel for re-engagement after a user has left our site. We will implement web push notifications, which requires careful frontend and backend coordination. On the frontend, we will use a Service Worker to manage the subscription and display of notifications, following best practices from MDN documentation. The backend team will need to build the infrastructure to securely trigger these messages for key events, such as application abandonment or important rate change announcements.

Primary Goal: Create a powerful, direct-to-user communication channel to recover lost leads and drive repeat traffic.

Measuring Success: Key Performance Indicators

Every step of the roadmap is tied to specific, measurable business outcomes to track ROI and guide future iterations.

Engagement KPIs

  • Conversion Rate Lift: Increase in leads/applications from pages with widgets vs. control.
  • Engagement Rate: % of users who interact with a utility widget.
  • Bounce Rate Reduction: Decrease in users leaving a page after seeing a helpful widget.

Efficiency KPIs

  • Campaign Deployment Time: Reduction in time for marketing to launch a new widget-based offer.
  • Offer Relevance Score: A/B test results comparing automated offers vs. manual assignments.
  • Widget Adoption Rate: Number of pages across the site utilizing the new CMS components.

Recovery & Re-Engagement KPIs

  • Application Recovery Rate: % of abandoned applications successfully recovered via web push notifications.
  • Click-Through Rate (CTR): % of users who click a push notification to return to the site.
  • Repeat Visits: Increase in site visits from the push notification subscriber segment.
